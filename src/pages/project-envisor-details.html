The Client

Envisor was a fintech startup based out of New York that aimed to create performance management software for asset managers with complex fund setups. With nearly $100 billion of assets under administration, Envisor handled the performance management reporting of some of the most demanding Hedge Funds, Private Equities, and Fund of Funds in the US, including Makena Capital Management, Sequoia Capital, and Commonfund. Investcloud acquired Envisor to integrate the performance management technologies into their broader industry tooling.

The Problem

Hedge Funds, Private Equities, and Fund of Funds invest in large numbers of investment vehicles each of which must be benchmarked against a known benchmark (e.g. S&P 500) individually and as part of a basket of other investment vehicles that changes over time. The quantity of data that is required to calculate this performance information is quite large and the accuracy of the reports is of critical importance. In addition to fund performance information, Envisor wanted to build a next generation user experience that allowed fund administrators to more quickly and easily navigate key concepts such as investment vehicle setup, fund valuation records, and cash flows.

The Solution

Envisor partnered with Quadratica to develop and maintain a data architecture that handles rapid daily growth (about .3GB per day of incoming financial records and associated metadata). The data architecture was developed in a way to review both historical performance and to model “what-if” scenarios. Simultaneously Envisor worked with Quadratica to outline the complex workflows and personas of the users of the system to enable the design and implementation (and iteration) of a user friendly back office interface to rapidly and correctly input investment vehicle information, fund valuation records, complex cash flow statements, and performance “tree” setups.

Performance Management

With a set of databases that grows by around 300MB per day based on incoming financial records, Quadratica set out to create a data architecture that would scale with the increased demands of the Envisor user base and the sheer number of funds that were managed in the system. By understanding the complex calculations that were being performed and the requirements of the end-users, Quadratica was able to create a scalable OLAP cube that could handle all of the “slice and dice” operations necessitated by the downstream software. In this domain space, it was quite common to need the results of one computation for a downstream computation, so the construction of the data architecture was a careful balancing act of memoizing just the right computations without completely blowing up the storage budget, whilst still maintaining high performance for all of the real-time and scheduling reporting necessitated by the downstream consumers. In addition, Quadratica setup High Availability and Disaster Recovery policies for the data storage tier and did regular tests on these systems to ensure that they functioned correctly.

First Class User Experience

Fund Administration for large scale Fund of Funds is a very complex domain with its own language, rules, and regulations. Without fully understanding the domain, it is very easy to create user experiences that look attractive on the surface, but are woefully incomplete. For example, while the concept of a cash flow is relatively well understood (move money from one account to another), for a fund of fund, there are a number of considerations that must be taken to ensure the cash flow records are correct. Concepts such as side-pocketing, one-sided or double-sided cash flows, and sub-cent accuracy increase both the design complexity and the technical complexity of building a UI that is fit for purpose. Quadratica worked tirelessly to understand the business domain to a degree where we could confidently plan out each building block and create reasonable implementation roadmaps for each feature without having to do rework in the future. A key takeaway of this process was the requirement of an “evidence based” workflow for any record that was entered in the system, allowing supervising personnel to quickly review the work of data entry personnel. Once the evidence based workflows were locked down, we worked to iteratively create the features and receive rapid feedback from key Envisor stakeholders so that we could pivot functionality as quickly as possible. This new evidence based workflow model drastically streamlined the Fund Administrator’s user experience. It also added built-in guardrails and reviewable checkpoints to the workflow to drastically reduce data entry errors.

Conclusion

Quadratica and Envisor set out on an ambitious plan to create a cutting-edge fund performance management system that would give unprecedented insight into how large scale hedge funds and fund of funds were performing on any given day. By taking the time to learn the ins and outs of the complex financial investment landscape, we were able to deliver a solution that made Envisor an attractive buy-out target for Investcloud in short order.
